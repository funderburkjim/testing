In the combat between the swans and the peacocks, in which equal valour was displayed, Ethe awans were deceived, their confidence being gained by the crows, having resided in the enemy's house. P. 62. The princes asked-How was that? Vishnusarmá related as
follows-There is, in Karpuradvipa, a lake, known as Padmakeli In it dwelt & royal swan, Hiranyagarbha by name. He wag installed their king by all the aquatic birds having assembled together. For,
If there be no king to guide the subjects properly, they would sink (in misery ) in this world, like a boat without & helmsman in the sea. Moreover,
The king protects the subjects; they enrich the king :
protection is better than enrichment: for in its absence, even what is, is not (i.., no property is secure).
One day that royal swan was sitting at ease on a large lotus-couch surrounded by his retinue, when a crane, Dîrghamukha by name, coming from some country, made his obeisance and sat down. Dirghamukha, said the king, you have come from a foreign country. Tell us the news. The crane replied, sire, there is great news and I have hastened here to communicate that. May Your Highness listen. There is & mountain Vindhys by name, in Jambudyipa, where lives & peacock Ohitravarna by name, king of birds.
