Karataka observed--Perhaps the lord might disregard you for your entrance (into his presence) at an inopportune time. He replied--Let it be so. Still a servant must necessarily be in the presence of his master. For,
Not beginning a thing through fear of fault (or, of an ill consequence ) is the sign of a coward; who, brother, leaves off taking food for fear of indigestion ? Bee
A king favours (or gets attached to ) & man who is near him, whether he be illiterate, low-born or unworthy of associetion (or, not attached to his master): as a general rule, kings, young women and creepers cling to (lit. twine round) him who is near them.
